Bad Companies

Tags: General

Soon after starting this blog I wrote posts on companies of which I have been a paying customer, but will never be a customer again and separately companies I love. The first post was much longer than the second. I’ve been meaning to write an update on those for some time, but never got around to it. Now it is time. As always no links to bad companies, they don’t deserve the slightest referral. Also, I’ve probably forgotten a bunch in the intervening years.

More companies of which I have been a paying customer, but will never be a customer again

  • Tango - They sell gift cards, supposedly.
  • Thomasz Developments - Real estate developers.
  • NYT Real Estate - is there a good real estate company? I’ve never found one.
  • McDonalds - This one has been on my naughty list since 1999, I just forgot last time.
  • Maylands Physio

Companies which I will try avoid

I am still customers of these companies (although pay them no money), but I wish I wasn’t, and am slowly working to remove them from my life.

  • Dropbox
  • HSBC - I have one account left with First Direct in the UK, it is still useful, but I’m looking at possible alternatives.
  • Microsoft - Only their size and ubiquity save this repeat offender. I am off Windows again (and back to Linux), hopefully that can continue.

Companies no longer loved

Neither of the previously loved companies get to maintain that level. No company has shown especially good customer service to me in recent years, so no company gets any sense of loyalty from me.

  • Amazon - Continually getting worse. I’d still buy from them, but definitely don’t consider their customer service to be nearly as good as before.
  • First direct - Part of HSBC, see above.
